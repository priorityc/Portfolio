Project Overview

Role

Front End Developer

Timeline

1 months

Responsibilities

UI engineering, front-end development, responsive layout, interaction design

Case Study

The London Museum of Science launched a thrilling new astronomy event and wanted a digital experience that felt magical. The museum needed a landing page that didn’t just inform, but pulled visitors to engagement. Something immersive, atmospheric, and family‑friendly. Their goal was to increase awareness, attract families, and encourage sign‑ups for the event.

Problem

Their existing website announcements weren’t capturing attention — visitors were scrolling past without feeling the excitement. The museum wanted something more immersive — a visually striking, interactive experience that would spark curiosity and encourage visitors to explore further.

Process

Research & Insights

Before designing the landing page, I explored how museums and science institutions typically present special events online. Many rely on static banners, long text blocks, or generic hero images that fail to create emotional engagement. This helped me identify several key insights:

  • Users respond strongly to visual storytelling
  • Museum websites often feel informational, not inspirational
  • Interactive elements increase engagement and conversions
  • The event theme should shape the entire experience

Hero section

Form CTA

Design Decisions

Instead of a static banner, I used motion to create an immediate sense of immersion. The swirling black hole animation acts as a visual anchor, pulling users into the experience and encouraging them to scroll.

Outcome & Lessons Learned

The final landing page successfully delivers an immersive, astronomy‑themed experience that captures users’ attention within the first few seconds. The dynamic black‑hole animation, combined with clear messaging and scroll‑triggered interactions, creates a sense of curiosity and excitement that aligns with the museum’s event. The design communicates key information quickly, making it easy for families to understand what the event is about and how to subscribe.

Built a Prototype for service quote calculator with Figma

Service Calculator

Storyboards PowerPoint Figma

Prototype for a construction quote calculator to speed up client estimates.

Demo Back to Projects

Project Overview

Role

Interaction Designer & UX

Timeline

6 months

Responsibilities

Research, Methods, Requirements, Storyboards, Prototype

Case Study

A small construction business needed a fast, reliable way to generate service quotes while working on the move. Manual calculations were slow and inconsistent, reducing client trust. This project explores a simple, mobile‑friendly solution that supports quick, transparent quoting during site visits.

Problem

The business owner manages all operations alone and has little time for manual calculations. Slow quoting leads to delays, inconsistent pricing, and lost client trust. A faster, more accurate solution was needed.

Process

Research & Insights

This project followed an iterative, user‑centred design approach: research → insights → prototype → feedback → refinement.

  • Context exploration
  • Understanding user environment
  • Observing real workflow
  • Identifying pain points

Design Decisions

The design prioritises quick, simple actions because the business owner works on site... Mobile-first flow: Designed for quick use during site visits. Step-by-step structure: Mirrors how contractors naturally assess jobs. Minimal input: Automatic measurements, simple selectors. Clear hierarchy: Large headings and spacing for fast scanning. Real-world terminology: Uses language contractors already use. Review screen: Builds trust and reduces errors.

Outcome & Lessons Learned

The final prototype delivers a clear, mobile‑friendly quoting flow that supports fast, on‑site use. It simplifies the contractor’s process, reduces manual input, and provides a structured way to capture essential details. The project strengthened my ability to translate real‑world workflows into intuitive digital interactions and helped me refine my approach to designing simple, efficient mobile experiences.
